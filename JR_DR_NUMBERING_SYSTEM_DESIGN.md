# JR/DR Numbering System Design

**Date:** November 2, 2025  
**Purpose:** Design a coherent numbering system for Jacqueline's and Daniel's answering affidavits that mirrors Peter's AD paragraph structure

---

## 1. Design Principles

### 1.1 Core Concept

**JR (Jacqueline Response)** and **DR (Daniel Response)** numbering will mirror the original AD (Applicant's Document) numbering to maintain:

1. **Traceability:** Each response paragraph directly maps to its corresponding AD paragraph
2. **Coherence:** Related responses from both affidavits use the same base number
3. **Clarity:** Sub-paragraphs indicate multiple points addressing the same AD paragraph
4. **Completeness:** All 141 AD paragraphs must be addressed

### 1.2 Numbering Format

**Format:** `[PREFIX] [AD_NUMBER].[SUB_NUMBER]`

**Examples:**
- AD 7.1 → JR 7.1 (Jacqueline's first response to AD 7.1)
- AD 7.1 → JR 7.1.1, JR 7.1.2 (Multiple points from Jacqueline on AD 7.1)
- AD 7.1 → DR 7.1 (Daniel's first response to AD 7.1)
- AD 7.1 → DR 7.1.1, DR 7.1.2 (Multiple points from Daniel on AD 7.1)

### 1.3 Cross-Reference Coherence

When Jacqueline and Daniel both address the same AD paragraph with multiple sub-points on the same subject matter:

- **JR 7.1.1** and **DR 7.1.1** both address the same aspect of AD 7.1
- **JR 7.1.2** and **DR 7.1.2** both address a second aspect of AD 7.1
- This creates **cognitive synergy** when reading both affidavits together

## 3. Response Paragraph Structure

### 3.1 Basic Response (Single Point)

**AD Paragraph:** AD 7.1 - "Applicant discovered irregularities on 15 May 2025"

**Jacqueline's Response:**
```
JR 7.1 I deny the allegation. The Applicant was aware of all financial matters as the sole trustee with absolute powers.
```

**Daniel's Response:**
```
DR 7.1 I deny the allegation. I provided comprehensive reports to the Applicant on 6 June 2025, demonstrating transparency.
```

### 3.2 Multiple Points (Sub-Paragraphs)

**AD Paragraph:** AD 7.10 - "Card cancellations caused service interruptions"

**Jacqueline's Response:**
```
JR 7.10.1 The Applicant cancelled 15 business cards on 7 June 2025, one day after Daniel provided comprehensive reports.

JR 7.10.2 This timing demonstrates a manufactured crisis and consciousness of guilt.

JR 7.10.3 I substituted personal cards to prevent business collapse, evidencing the Applicant's bad faith.
```

**Daniel's Response:**
```
DR 7.10.1 I provided comprehensive reports to Bantjies on 6 June 2025, demonstrating cooperation.

DR 7.10.2 The Applicant cancelled cards on 7 June 2025, immediately after receiving my reports.

DR 7.10.3 This constitutes retaliation for exposing financial irregularities.
```

### 3.3 Coherent Sub-Paragraphs (Same Subject Matter)

When JR and DR sub-paragraphs address the same aspect:

**JR 7.10.1** and **DR 7.10.1** both address the **timing** of events  
**JR 7.10.2** and **DR 7.10.2** both address the **manufactured crisis**  
**JR 7.10.3** and **DR 7.10.3** both address the **retaliation**

This creates synergy when reading both affidavits together.

## 4. Section Headers

### 4.1 Major Section Headers (H3)

Format: `### [SECTION_NUMBER]. RESPONSE TO AD PARAGRAPHS [RANGE]: [DESCRIPTION]`

**Example:**
```markdown
### 6. RESPONSE TO AD PARAGRAPHS 7.1-7.20: DISCOVERY OF FINANCIAL IRREGULARITIES
```

### 4.2 Subsection Headers (H4) - Optional

For complex sections, use subsection headers to group related AD paragraphs:

**Example:**
```markdown
#### Response to AD 7.1-7.5: Timeline of Discovery
#### Response to AD 7.6-7.10: Card Cancellations
```

## 5. Implementation Guidelines

### 5.1 Paragraph Numbering Rules

1. **Every AD paragraph must be addressed** - even if only to confirm or deny
2. **Use the exact AD number** as the base for JR/DR numbering
3. **Add sub-numbers (.1, .2, .3)** when making multiple points
4. **Maintain sequential order** following Peter's affidavit structure
5. **Cross-reference** related JR/DR paragraphs when appropriate

### 5.2 Content Guidelines

1. **Factual and neutral tone** - no hyperbole or speculation
2. **Evidence-based** - cite specific annexures (e.g., DF1, JF2)
3. **Concise** - each paragraph addresses one clear point
4. **Coherent** - related sub-paragraphs flow logically
5. **Synergistic** - JR and DR paragraphs complement each other

### 5.3 Annexure References

Format: `(Annexure [PREFIX][NUMBER])`

- Jacqueline: **JF1, JF2, JF3**, etc.
- Daniel: **DF1, DF2, DF3**, etc.
- Shared: **SF1, SF2, SF3**, etc.
